Understanding the Treatment

What is Ketamine Therapy?

A Breakthrough in Mental Health Treatment

Ketamine therapy works differently than traditional antidepressants. While conventional medications can take weeks or months to show effects, ketamine often provides relief within 24-48 hours.

Originally used as an anesthetic, ketamine has been shown to rapidly reduce symptoms by targeting NMDA receptors in the brain and promoting neural plasticity, your brain's ability to form new connections and heal.

Our supervised IV infusions are administered in a calm, comfortable setting with continuous medical monitoring throughout your session.

What We Treat

Our Primary Focus Areas

Ketamine therapy has shown the most remarkable effectiveness for two primary conditions.

🧠

Treatment-Resistant Depression

Ketamine offers a new pathway to relief through a completely different mechanism than traditional antidepressants. Particularly effective for:

  • Depression with hypersomnia (oversleeping)
  • Seasonal affective disorder
  • Bipolar depression
  • Depression that hasn't responded to multiple medications
  • Suicidal ideation in crisis situations
🎖️

PTSD & Trauma

Ketamine is highly effective at dialing down the autonomic fight-or-flight response and pulling people out of the parasympathetic freeze state. Particularly effective for:

  • Combat-related PTSD (we honor veterans with 20% discount)
  • Complex trauma with hypervigilance
  • Dissociative freeze states
  • Trauma-fueled anxiety
  • Treatment-resistant trauma symptoms

What About Other Conditions?

While ketamine has shown promise for conditions like premenstrual syndrome and autistic burnout, our primary expertise and focus remains treatment-resistant depression and trauma. During your consultation, we'll provide an honest assessment of whether ketamine is likely to help you.

Your Journey

What to Expect

Every step of your treatment is designed for comfort, safety, and effectiveness.

Before Your Session

Initial Consultation: We'll review your medical history, current medications, substance use, and treatment goals. We'll provide an honest assessment of whether you're an excellent candidate, a 50/50 candidate, or not yet ready for ketamine therapy.

Pre-Treatment Optimization: Sometimes we'll recommend addressing medication issues or substance use before starting ketamine. This ensures the best possible outcome.

Preparation: Avoid eating 4 hours before your appointment. Arrange for someone to drive you home. Wear comfortable clothing.

During Your Session

Setting: You'll relax in a private, comfortable room with dimmed lighting and calming music. Many patients use eye masks and headphones.

Administration: The ketamine infusion lasts 40-60 minutes. You'll remain awake but may feel deeply relaxed or have mild dissociative sensations. Our medical team monitors you continuously with vitals checks every 15 minutes.

Experience: Most patients describe a peaceful, introspective state. Some report visual or sensory shifts. All effects are temporary and resolve shortly after the infusion ends.

After Your Session

Recovery: You'll rest in our clinic for 20-30 minutes as the effects wear off. Most patients feel normal within 1-2 hours.

Results: Many patients notice improvement within 24 hours. Effects build over the course of your treatment series. We'll schedule follow-up appointments to track your progress and adjust your protocol as needed.

Next Steps: Rest at home for the remainder of the day. No driving, operating machinery, or making major decisions for 24 hours.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you determine if I'm a good candidate?

During your consultation, we'll review your psychiatric history, current medications, substance use, and specific symptoms. We place you in one of three categories: excellent candidate, 50/50 candidate (with factors working against you), or not yet ready. We believe in honest assessment over maximizing revenue.

What if I'm not ready for ketamine yet?

Sometimes we recommend addressing other issues first, like optimizing your medication regimen, reducing substance use, or working with a therapist. Ketamine works best when other factors are stable. We'd rather have you succeed later than fail now.

Is ketamine therapy safe?

Yes. Ketamine has been used safely in medical settings for over 50 years. At the doses used for mental health treatment, side effects are minimal and temporary. All sessions are medically supervised with continuous monitoring.

How quickly will I see results?

Many patients notice improvement within 24 hours of their first infusion. The full benefits typically build over the course of the 6-session induction series. Individual results vary.

How long do the effects last?

After the initial induction series, most patients maintain results with periodic booster sessions every 2-8 weeks. The frequency is personalized based on your response and needs.

Does insurance cover ketamine therapy?

Most insurance plans do not currently cover IV ketamine infusions for mental health. However, we provide documentation you can submit for potential reimbursement. We also offer veteran discounts and flexible payment options.

Can I continue my current medications?

In most cases, yes. Ketamine works through a different mechanism than traditional antidepressants. However, sometimes we'll recommend optimizing your medications before starting ketamine for best results. We'll review everything during your consultation.

Do I need to stop drinking or using marijuana?

Heavy alcohol or marijuana use significantly reduces ketamine's effectiveness. If this applies to you, we'll discuss a plan to address it before starting treatment. Think of ketamine as fertilizer in the garden of you. Substance use is poison in that garden.
