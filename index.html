Why This Clinic Exists

In West Tennessee, the mental health system has a gap you could drive 90 miles through. That's exactly how far patients have been driving to access comprehensive psychiatric care. For people already struggling to get out of bed, that distance isn't just inconvenient. It's a barrier that keeps them from getting better.

Claritiv Health was founded by someone who saw this broken system firsthand. Not from a textbook, but from life. The endless medication carousel. The six-week waiting periods for drugs that might not work. The quiet desperation of watching people you love suffer while the system shrugs and says "give it time."

This facility exists because West Tennessee deserves access to comprehensive mental health treatment that actually works. Without the drive, without the wait, and without the stigma. We're not here to replace your doctor or your therapist. We're here to provide complete psychiatric care when the conventional playbook has run out of pages.

Absolutely. Ketamine has been FDA-approved as an anesthetic since 1970 and has an outstanding safety profile spanning over five decades of clinical use. It is on the World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines and is used safely in hospitals worldwide every single day.

At Claritiv Health, all infusions are administered by licensed providers in a fully monitored clinical setting. Your heart rate, blood pressure, and oxygen levels are tracked continuously throughout the entire session. Our team is trained in advanced cardiac life support, and we maintain emergency protocols that exceed standard safety guidelines.

The sub-anesthetic doses used for mood disorders are significantly lower than those used in surgical settings, making the treatment gentle and well-tolerated by the vast majority of patients.

The typical protocol is 6 infusion sessions over 2-3 weeks for the initial series. This loading phase allows ketamine to build up its neuroplastic effects, essentially rewiring neural pathways that have been damaged by chronic depression, anxiety, or trauma. Each session builds upon the last.

After completing the initial series, most patients transition to monthly maintenance infusions to sustain their improvement. Some patients find they can extend the time between maintenance sessions over time, while others prefer a consistent monthly schedule. We'll create a personalized plan based on your unique response to treatment.

When you arrive, you'll be welcomed into a private treatment suite designed for maximum comfort. Soft lighting, a weighted blanket, noise-canceling headphones with curated ambient music. Every detail is intentional. Your provider will place a small IV catheter, and the infusion begins slowly over 40 to 60 minutes.

Most patients describe a deep sense of relaxation, lightness, and warmth within the first 10 to 15 minutes. You may experience mild dissociation, a gentle feeling of floating or detachment from your body, which many patients describe as pleasant, meditative, or even profoundly meaningful. Colors may seem more vivid, and you might experience a sense of perspective or emotional distance from your pain.

Throughout the entire experience, you remain conscious and can communicate with your care team at any time. After the infusion, you'll rest in our recovery area for about 30 minutes while the effects gently subside. Most patients describe feeling a remarkable lightness, as if a weight has been quietly lifted from their shoulders.

Your first visit begins with a comprehensive consultation where we review your medical history, current medications, and treatment goals. This is a judgment-free conversation. We want to understand your journey and what you've been through. We'll explain the science behind ketamine therapy, walk you through exactly what will happen, and answer every question you have.

If you're approved for treatment, your first infusion typically takes place the same week. Plan to arrive about 15 minutes early to settle in. We recommend wearing comfortable clothing, eating a light meal 2 to 3 hours beforehand, and arranging for someone to drive you home. The entire visit, from check-in through recovery, takes approximately 90 minutes to 2 hours.

Many patients are nervous before their first session, and that's completely normal. Our team is exceptionally attentive to first-time patients. We'll check in with you frequently, adjust the dose if needed, and ensure you feel safe and supported throughout the entire experience.

Ketamine therapy is remarkably well-tolerated, but like any medical treatment, some patients experience mild, temporary side effects. The most common include light nausea, mild dizziness, and a temporary increase in blood pressure during the infusion. We proactively manage nausea with anti-nausea medication, and most patients report that these effects diminish significantly after the first session or two.

During the infusion itself, you may experience perceptual changes: things like vivid colors, a sense of time distortion, or feeling as though you're gently floating. These are expected effects of the dissociative properties of ketamine and are actually associated with better treatment outcomes. They typically resolve within 15 to 30 minutes after the infusion ends.

Some patients feel slightly tired or "spacey" for a few hours after treatment, which is why we require a designated driver. By the next morning, the vast majority of patients feel completely normal. Many report feeling significantly better than they have in months or even years.

Traditional antidepressants like SSRIs and SNRIs work by adjusting serotonin and norepinephrine levels, a process that takes 4 to 8 weeks to produce noticeable effects, and only works for about 60 to 70% of patients. For the remaining 30 to 40%, these medications simply don't provide adequate relief, leading to years of trial and error with different drugs and dosages.

Ketamine works through an entirely different mechanism. It targets the glutamate system, the brain's most abundant neurotransmitter, and rapidly promotes the growth of new synaptic connections through a process called neuroplasticity. Think of it as helping your brain rebuild pathways that depression, anxiety, or trauma have damaged. This is why many patients notice improvement within hours rather than weeks.

Perhaps most importantly, ketamine has shown remarkable efficacy in patients who haven't responded to traditional treatments. Studies show response rates of 70% or higher in treatment-resistant populations, people who had lost hope after years of failed medications. It's not replacing your other treatments; it's offering a new pathway when the conventional ones have fallen short.
